The Story
So Far

I'm a design researcher who likes the messy middle of how people work together. After a bachelor's in industrial design in Beijing and a master's in strategic design in Germany, I spent my PhD at Eindhoven University of Technology studying how design teams co-explore — especially when they're apart.

I work across qualitative and quantitative methods, turn complex data into stories people can act on, and care most about keeping the human at the centre of the decision.

Recent

This year I graduated. I successfully defended Co-Exploration in Remote Design Collaboration at Eindhoven University of Technology on 14 January 2026, and — with the approval of a committee drawn from universities across the world — I'm now entitled to use the title Dr.

I designed the book myself: the cover, all the illustrations, and the writing. It pulls together the papers I published across my PhD, investigating how design teams build on each other's ideas while working remotely.

In my spare time I'm exploring AI-driven tools for design work, and picking up an old favourite hobby again: painting.
